Optimization of medical websites using inclusive UX design principles

Tech: Figma, HTML, JavaScript and CSS

My bachelor's thesis examined how the medical website of the Center for Integrative Psychiatry could be made more accessible and usable by applying inclusive user experience design principles. The aim was to identify existing barriers and provide suitable solutions based on inclusive, human-centred design principles. The theoretical framework was formed using relevant standards, laws and guidelines, such as the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines and the Barrier-Free Information Technology Ordinance, as well as inclusive design principles. The study employed qualitative methods, including interviews, usability tests, heuristic evaluations, and simulations. It was found that inclusive and human-centred design principles can transform users' perceptions of medical websites, shifting their view from "These big, fat blocks of text are annoying" to "This looks great now!"
